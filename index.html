More About Economic Inequality

Economic inequality refers to the unequal distribution of income and opportunity among different social groups. It is a global problem and many people are trapped in poverty with very few opportunities to move up the social ladder and live a better life, but we are not the only ones who acknowlege the danger of this issue. There are a multitude of organizations that fight against poverty and advocate for financial reform. 

RESULTS

RESULTS is a social effort of passionate people. They use their collective voices to influence political decisions that will end poverty. Volunteers are trained, supported, and inspired to become proactive. Volunteers eventually learn to effectively advise policymakers, guiding them toward decisions that improve access to education and economic opportunity. These organizations enable people to make a difference in their community by banding together, contacting state legislators, and organizing advocacy events. You, too, can get involved and make a difference.

The Borgen Project

The Borgen Project seeks to raise awareness of global poverty and the problems it causes. The Borgen Project, through advocacy and campaigning, forces the nation's leaders to pay attention to this global issue and encourages action to address it in US foreign policy. The Borgen Project offers volunteer and internship opportunities.  Anyone can volunteer since it has positions in Seattle and also online.

endPoverty

endPoverty is a global, faith-based organization that provides hardworking men and women with economic and spiritual resources to help them unleash their potential and break free from the shackles of poverty. Private enterprise and job creation can transform impoverished communities. Entrepreneurs learn the skills needed to start or grow businesses using best practices in business management through strategic capital lending, business training, and coaching. endPoverty teaches them what it takes to become a business leader and make a difference in their families and communities.

The NCLEJ

The National Center for Law and Economic Justice is a non-profit organization that provides legal representation and policy advocacy throughout the United States. They help low-income families, individuals, and communities across the country gain access to more opportunities to improve their well-being through racial and economic justice. You can participate by donating.
